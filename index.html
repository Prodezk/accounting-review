01  /  Executive Summary

Executive Summary

This document presents the findings of an internal operational review of the Accounting Department at Prodezk Inc. The review was conducted with the intent to identify opportunities for strengthening the department's processes, service standards, leadership effectiveness, and team dynamics — and to translate those opportunities into a concrete, time-bound improvement roadmap.

The accounting function is a cornerstone of Prodezk's service delivery and client trust. The insights captured in this review reflect a team with real capability and foundational knowledge. What this plan addresses are the structural and operational conditions that, when improved, will allow that capability to express itself fully and consistently.

This is not a disciplinary document. It is a leadership investment — a commitment to giving the Accounting Manager and his team the clarity, tools, and expectations they need to perform at the level this company requires and this team is capable of reaching.

02  /  Purpose

Purpose of This Review

Growing companies regularly assess their departments not to find fault, but to ensure that their teams are equipped with the right structure, clarity, and support to scale alongside the business. This review serves exactly that purpose.

The Accounting Department has been operating in a high-demand environment. This document acknowledges that context and responds to it — not by adding pressure, but by introducing structure, clarity, and a shared direction that will make the team's work more efficient, more satisfying, and more impactful.

The Accounting Manager is expected to use this plan as a living operational document — reviewing it regularly, tracking progress, and communicating updates to leadership.

03  /  Key Themes

Key Operational Themes Requiring Attention

The review surfaced five interconnected themes. Each represents a concrete lever for improvement and a clear opportunity for the department to operate more effectively.

📋

Workload Planning & Task Distribution

Task allocation and time management practices need formalization. Timelines, ownership, and sequencing are the foundation of a high-performing team.

💬

Client Communication Standards

Client responsiveness and meeting documentation are areas where the team can build stronger client trust and reduce misunderstandings significantly.

🎯

Leadership & Active Management

The Accounting Manager's role needs to expand from reactive support to proactive planning, metrics tracking, and team development facilitation.

🤝

Team Climate & Engagement

Team morale, internal communication, and a culture of psychological safety are essential for retention, cohesion, and consistent high performance.

⚙️

Process Maturity & Quality Control

Standardized accounting criteria and internal quality controls will reduce variability in output and strengthen Prodezk's credibility with clients.

04  /  Priorities

Recommended Improvement Priorities

The following priorities are ranked by their organizational impact and their potential to unlock compounding improvements across the department:

1

Establish a formal task planning and assignment system

Introduce weekly planning cycles with defined deliverables, deadlines, and ownership per accountant. Eliminate ad-hoc prioritization that displaces committed work.

2

Define and enforce client communication response standards

Set maximum response time targets for emails, WhatsApp, and calls. Equip the team with standardized response protocols and tools accessible from the desktop.

3

Shift the manager's operating model toward proactive leadership

Transition from primarily resolving escalations to running regular 1:1s, team check-ins, and performance metric reviews — creating a culture of structured accountability.

4

Standardize accounting criteria and internal documentation

Create a unified accounting criteria playbook so the team speaks with one voice to clients — preventing contradictory guidance that erodes trust.

5

Rebuild team engagement and internal communication practices

Reintroduce team rituals, ensure organizational decisions are communicated proactively, and create a climate where team members feel informed, valued, and secure.

05  /  Team Development

Team Development & Accountability Expectations

A high-performing accounting team does not emerge from technical skill alone. It requires clarity of expectations, ongoing development, and a culture where accountability is embraced rather than feared. The following commitments are expected at both the individual and team level.

🎯

Individual Ownership

Each accountant is expected to own their client portfolio: plan proactively, flag risks early, and deliver on commitments without requiring escalation for routine matters.

📊

Performance Visibility

Key metrics — task completion rates, response times, client satisfaction signals — will be tracked and reviewed monthly. What gets measured improves.

🗣️

Open Communication

Concerns about workload, tools, or team dynamics should be raised early, directly, and constructively — not absorbed silently or expressed externally.

📚

Continuous Learning

The team is expected to develop professionally — staying current with accounting standards, client communication best practices, and the tools Prodezk uses.

🤝

Collaborative Coverage

Dependencies on specific individuals for critical closings must be resolved. The team will cross-train to ensure operational continuity regardless of availability.

⭐

Professional Standards

Every client interaction — email, meeting, call — is a reflection of Prodezk. The team will be held to a professional standard that builds long-term client relationships.

06  /  Process & Quality

Process & Quality Control Enhancements

Operational excellence in accounting depends on consistent, documented processes that reduce variability and build client confidence. The following process improvements are required:

✓

Weekly Planning Cadence

Each accountant submits a structured weekly plan every Monday identifying their priorities, client deadlines, and any anticipated blockers. The manager reviews and approves.

✓

Meeting Documentation Protocol

Every client meeting or call must be followed by a written summary (email or shared memo) documenting agreements, action items, and next steps — sent within 24 hours.

✓

Unified Accounting Criteria Document

The Accounting Manager will lead the creation of a shared criteria document that defines how specific scenarios are handled — ensuring consistency across the team and eliminating contradictory client guidance.

✓

Escalation Path Clarity

Define a clear, lightweight escalation protocol. Routine tasks should not require Joseph or Daniel's involvement. A documented decision tree will reduce delays and build team autonomy.

✓

Workload Equity Reviews

The manager will conduct monthly workload reviews to ensure equitable distribution of client portfolios. Complexity, volume, and available time will all be factored into assignments.

✓

Client Communication Tools Audit

Identify gaps in the team's access to communication tools (Sales IQ, WhatsApp desktop access, etc.) and resolve them. The team should not be limited by tooling constraints.

07  /  Service & Communication

Service, Responsiveness & Internal Communication Standards

Client service is not separate from accounting work — it is the work. How the team communicates with clients directly determines Prodezk's reputation and client retention. The following table contrasts current observed patterns with the expected standard.

⚠️ Current Pattern

  • Client emails and messages treated as lower priority than task execution
  • Delayed responses to WhatsApp and calls during the workday
  • Verbal agreements made in meetings with no written follow-up
  • Uncertainty about how to proactively engage clients on sensitive requests
  • Account access issues creating friction in client service
  • Team unaware of remote work days until day-of

✅ Expected Standard

  • Client communications acknowledged within 2 hours; resolved within 24 hours
  • All meetings followed by a written summary within 24 hours
  • Proactive outreach to clients on pending information — using scripts when needed
  • Remote work and schedule changes communicated at least 48 hours in advance
  • Account access issues escalated and resolved within 48 hours
  • Team briefed on all organizational decisions that affect them — no surprises
08  /  Leadership

Leadership Expectations for the Accounting Manager

The Accounting Manager's role is pivotal. This review reflects an investment in that role — an expectation that the position evolves from technical lead to operational manager who drives planning, accountability, team development, and client excellence simultaneously.

The following expectations define what effective leadership in this role looks like going forward:

01

Plan Proactively — Don't React

The manager is responsible for anticipating workload peaks, distributing tasks in advance, and ensuring the team is never caught off-guard by deadlines. Weekly planning is non-negotiable.

02

Build a Culture of Accountability with Psychological Safety

The team should receive regular, constructive feedback — not silence. Create an environment where mistakes are learning opportunities and high performance is recognized openly.

03

Communicate Organizational Decisions Promptly

When decisions affecting the team are made — staffing changes, remote policies, process updates — the manager communicates them immediately and with context. No team member should hear important news secondhand.

04

Standardize the Team's Technical Voice

Inconsistent accounting criteria between team members is a reputational risk. The manager will lead alignment sessions to ensure every team member applies the same standards and communicates the same positions to clients.

05

Champion Team Development and Wellbeing

Reintroduce team rituals, facilitate skills development, and actively manage team morale. A manager who invests in their team earns consistent, sustainable performance in return.

06

Report Upward with Structure

Monthly updates to leadership on team performance, open risks, and progress against this improvement plan will be expected. The manager is the link between the team and the organization.

09  /  Improvement Plan

30 / 60 / 90 Day Improvement Plan

The following plan provides a structured, achievable timeline for implementing the improvements identified in this review. Progress will be reviewed at each milestone.

Days 1 – 30  |  Foundation
Establish Structure & Clarity
Launch weekly planning cycles — every accountant submits a structured work plan each Monday by 9:00 AM.
Conduct a workload equity review and rebalance portfolio assignments based on complexity and available capacity.
Define and communicate client response time standards in writing; share with the full team.
Introduce the meeting documentation protocol — all client meetings followed by a written summary within 24 hours.
Audit communication tools (Sales IQ, WhatsApp) and resolve access limitations blocking the team from serving clients efficiently.
Schedule individual 1:1s with each team member to align on expectations and hear their perspective.
Days 31 – 60  |  Standardization
Build Consistency & Team Alignment
Complete and publish the unified accounting criteria playbook; run an alignment session with the full team.
Define the escalation path for client closings — eliminating single-person dependencies on Joseph and Daniel for routine matters.
Launch a team engagement initiative: resume regular team rituals, whether brief weekly check-ins, a monthly team lunch, or a structured retrospective.
Review and address salary equity concerns through a structured conversation with leadership — ensuring fairness is visible to the team.
Implement first monthly performance review cycle — track task completion rates, client response adherence, and escalation frequency.
Days 61 – 90  |  Elevation
Measure, Reinforce & Scale
Conduct a full 90-day review of all improvement metrics — present findings to leadership with honest assessment of progress.
Celebrate wins explicitly — communicate improvements that have been achieved to reinforce the team's trajectory.
Identify any remaining gaps and develop a Phase 2 improvement plan for the following quarter.
Evaluate the onboarding of new team members (including Katherine) and identify any integration or alignment actions needed.
Establish the department's KPI dashboard — a living view of team performance visible to both the manager and leadership.

A Strong Department Starts Here

The Accounting Department at Prodezk holds a critical position in the company's ability to serve clients with excellence and sustain business growth. The team has the foundation — the knowledge, the experience, and the commitment to the work.

What this plan adds is structure, clarity, and direction. With the right systems in place and a manager who leads proactively, the department has every reason to become one of Prodezk's strongest assets — a team that clients trust, that leadership relies on, and that its members are proud to be part of.

This review is not a conclusion. It is a starting line.
